About

As you might’ve found out from above, my name is Bryan Wendlandt and I am now wrapping up my final semester for my undergraduate degrees.

For the past seven months and counting, I have been lucky enough to be a part of Intel’s Internet of Things Group, specifically in retail segments. I’ve been a part of a huge variety of projects from hands-on development of PoCs to on-site customer visits to building hardware kits to writing and recording marketing materials and so much more. However, my history doesn’t stop there and you can find out even more about my career path in the sections below or in my résumé.

With a wide range of experience in both business and technical roles, I bring a unique perspective to any team. Going forward, my goal is to continue to take each new learning opportunity in stride and be able to directly contribute them to the new and innovative technologies that are transforming how many businesses operate today.

I’ve lived in the valley my entire life and love to take advantage of the great weather through hiking trails, kayaking up north, and spending time with my dog, Sienna. But, there is no doubt in my mind that the biggest advantage of living in Phoenix, Arizona is that a getaway to San Diego is only a short(ish) drive away.
